# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Security engineers, SOC analysts, CISOs, and IT architects deploying in air-gapped environments: defense contractors, government agencies, critical infrastructure operators, and regulated financial institutions. They work in high-stakes, high-accountability contexts. They know their tooling deeply and distrust marketing noise. When they evaluate new software they read the architecture page first; they check for telemetry before anything else.

## Product Purpose

AI SOC is an autonomous, multi-agent Security Operations Centre packaged as an Electron desktop app (.exe / .dmg / .AppImage) for fully air-gapped on-premise deployment. The marketing site's job is to convince a skeptical security professional that this is the real thing: technically capable, honestly described, and worth a download. Success is a confirmed download, a trial start, or an enterprise inquiry.

## Brand Personality

Powerful · Trustworthy · Open

The voice is a senior engineer who has shipped production systems in classified environments. Confident without being loud. Specific rather than promotional. The Apache-2.0 open-source core signals transparency; the design should reinforce it rather than override it with decoration.

References that capture the right register: HashiCorp (engineering credibility, technical authority without hype), Tailscale (open-core trust, clarity over sales), Wazuh (security-native, no consumer softening).

## Anti-references

- **Generic dark AI SaaS**: Purple-gradient hero + cyan glow cards + identical icon-heading-text card grid. The 2024 AI tool default. Every visual move on this site should be explainable in terms of content and architecture, not in terms of "looks technical."
- **Consumer startup**: Rounded-corner softness, pastel accents, playful microcopy. Wrong register for operators managing classified networks.
- **Heavy enterprise blue**: Crowdstrike / Palo Alto navy + gradient CTA hero. Dated, over-produced, zero open-source feel.

## Design Principles

1. **Technical honesty**: Every visual element earns its place by communicating real information. Decorative gradients and glow effects add no signal; a precise layout does. Architecture diagrams and real command output beat a hero-metric stat bar.
2. **Specificity over spectacle**: Concrete numbers, real pipeline stages, genuine terminal output. Copy and visuals answer "how does it actually work?" not "is this impressive?"
3. **Open-source gravity**: The Apache-2.0 core is a trust signal. The design should feel like something the community would contribute to: readable, reviewable, no decoration that obscures the underlying work.
4. **Earned hierarchy**: Visual weight reflects actual importance. Primary actions are clearly primary; sections are not inflated with glow and eyebrows to seem significant when the content already warrants attention.
5. **Operator-native calm**: Users maintain air-gapped systems next to Wazuh dashboards, Grafana panels, and terminals. The site should feel at home in that environment: high-information density where it earns it, no hype.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. The audience uses calibrated monitors in security operations centers; high contrast is a feature, not a fallback. Standard keyboard navigation and screen reader compatibility required.
